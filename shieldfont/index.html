— FAQ
01Will Shield Font break my SEO?
Yes — partly. Search engines index your body text for topic relevance and pull snippets from it for the results page, so wrapped paragraphs will rank against the decoy words instead of the real ones. Title tags, meta descriptions, headings, structured data, image alt text, and aria labels all stay readable to crawlers as long as you do not wrap them. Use Shield Font where reach matters less than protection: editorial writing, paywalled content, manifestos, or pages you would rather humans find through links than through search.
02What about screen readers?
Two-part answer. First: every Shield element ships aria-hidden=true by default, so screen readers skip the encoded gibberish entirely instead of reading it aloud as nonsense. Second: do NOT mirror the original sentence into aria-label — that text is in the HTML source and gets scraped too, defeating the protection. The right escape hatch for assistive access is a Listen to this article button driven by the browser speechSynthesis API, fed from the original text at build time. Shield Font body copy is less screen-reader friendly than plain text — plan that audio alternative up front if your audience needs assistive access.
03Does this stop AI training?
It poisons the input. In our V3 benchmark, a Qwen 2.5 3B model fine-tuned on a ten percent mix of Shield Font encoded text shows a plus zero point one zero nine composite drop in MMLU and substitute-word probe accuracy compared to a control trained on the same total amount of clean Wikipedia. Not zero, but enough to make the corpus a liability rather than a benefit. See the white paper for the full sweep across eleven mappings.
04Why does my source look like gibberish?
That is the point. Shield Font swaps your words for a curated dictionary of plausible-looking decoys. The font GSUB ligatures map the decoys back to glyphs shaped like your originals. Humans see English; scrapers see decoys.
05Will it work in Word, Pages, InDesign?
Yes — the font installs system-wide. Run your text through the encoder, paste the encoded version into your document, apply Shield Font Optik, export to PDF.
